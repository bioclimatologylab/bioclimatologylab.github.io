People

  • Park Williams

    Principal Investigator

    CV

    Park Williams is a bioclimatologist at Columbia University's Lamont-Doherty Earth Observatory. Park's research straddles the fields of climatology and ecology and he is especially interested in the climatological causes and the ecological consequences of drought. His research aims to improve understanding of drought and its effects on terrestrial systems, including forests, the carbon cycle, agriculture, and humanity.

  • Daniel Bishop

    Graduate Student

    CV

    Daniel's research interests broadly include bioclimatology and climate dynamics, specifically investigating the topics of drought, carbon sequestration in forest ecosystems, and land-atmosphere feedbacks. His current research focuses on evaluating spatial and temporal hydroclimate variability over North America.
